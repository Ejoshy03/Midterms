Geography of Surigao

Geographically, Surigao is characterized by its rugged terrain, with mountain ranges, hills, and valleys dominating much of its landscape. The province is bordered by the Philippine Sea to the east, which provides ample opportunities for fishing and water-related activities. To the west lies the province of Agusan del Norte, while Surigao del Sur borders it to the south. Surigao features mountainous regions such as the Cantilan Mountain Range and the Mabahin Mountain Range. These mountains are interspersed with fertile valleys and plains, where agriculture thrives. The province is also dotted with rivers and waterways, which serve as vital lifelines for irrigation and transportation.
